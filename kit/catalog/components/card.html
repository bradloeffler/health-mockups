Card

The primary content surface. Wraps a single block of related information. Comes in three weights — default, accent (for the day's marquee block), and quiet (for content that shouldn't compete).

Anatomy

A card is a padded surface with a hairline border and a soft shadow. Content sits on a baseline grid inside.

Eyebrow trailing
7,420
  • Surface background --c-surface, border --c-hair, shadow --sh-card.
  • Padding var(--s-3) on all sides — content never touches the edge.
  • Radius --r-lg (16px).
  • Inner highlight 1px inset top highlight via ::before for tactile depth.

Variants

Three weights. Use accent for the single most important block on a screen. Use quiet for filler/grouping where the card chrome would add noise.

Footnote
Quiet cards drop the surface and shadow so nested groups can breathe.

States

Cards themselves are not interactive primitives, but they receive hover/pressed treatments when wrapped in a button or anchor. The token-driven shadow gives the depth cue.

default
Default resting card.
hover (desktop only)
Hover lifts the shadow and strengthens the border.
pressed
Pressed scales down 1.5% and dims slightly.
